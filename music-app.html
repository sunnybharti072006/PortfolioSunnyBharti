System Architecture

The Music Player App follows a layered architecture with clear separation of concerns:

Activities, Fragments, and UI components implementing Material Design principles

Business Logic Layer

ViewModels, Use Cases, and Audio Service management with lifecycle awareness

Audio Engine Layer

Data Layer

Crossfade Audio Technology

The crossfade feature creates seamless transitions between tracks for uninterrupted listening:

Track A Ending
↓
Fade Out
↓
Abrupt Stop
Crossfade Process
Track A + Track B
↓
Blended Transition
↓
Seamless Experience

How Crossfade Works:

  • Blends the ending of current track with beginning of next track
  • Creates smooth transitions between songs of different genres and tempos

Project Outcome

This project showcases my deep understanding of audio signal processing, Android development lifecycle, and user-centered design principles. The Music Player App successfully delivers an enhanced listening experience for music enthusiasts.

Through this project, I implemented complex DSP Algo for real-time audio manipulation while maintaining optimal performance on mobile devices. The crossfade feature effectively creates seamless transitions between tracks, eliminating disruptive pauses during playback.

The application demonstrates professional-grade code architecture, efficient memory management, and seamless integration of multiple Android components including MediaPlayer, AudioFocus, and background services.

Technical Achievements:

  • Implemented real-time audio processing with Low latency
  • Designed custom crossfade algorithm
  • Achieved 60 FPS smooth UI with complex audio operations
  • Optimized battery consumption during continuous playback
  • Integrated with Android MediaStore for seamless file access
